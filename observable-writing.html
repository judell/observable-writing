In Knowledge Work as Craft Work (2002), Jim McGee wrote:

The journey from apprentice to master craftsman depends on the visibility of all aspects of craft work.

That was the inspiration for a talk I gave at the 2010 Traction User Group meeting, which focused on the theme of observable work. In the GitHub era we take for granted that we can craft software in the open, subjecting each iteration to highly granular anaysis and discussion. Beautiful Code (2007) invited accomplished programmers to explain their thinking. I imagine an annotated tour of GitHub repositories as the foundation of a future edition of that book.

I imagine the same kind of approach to crafting prose and then explaining the process.
